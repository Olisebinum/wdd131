Web Design & Development 131 – Design Principles

Design Principles

Principles of Design

Article

Proximity

Items that are related should be grouped together. When several items are in close proximity to each other, they become one visual unit rather than several separate units.

Alignment

Nothing should be placed on the page arbitrarily. Every element should have a visual connection with something else on the page. This creates a clean, sophisticated look.

Repetition

Repeat some aspect of the design throughout the entire piece. The repetition of elements strengthens the unity of the design and develops the organization and strengthens the unity.

Contrast

Contrast is often the most important visual attraction on a page. It creates an interesting page and aids in the organization of information.

List

  • Use a clear visual hierarchy
  • Limit your font choices to one or two
  • Use whitespace intentionally
  • Ensure sufficient color contrast
  • Align elements consistently
  • Group related content together
  • Repeat design elements for unity
  • Test on multiple screen sizes

Key Principles

Good design is not just about how things look, but how they work. Applying core principles of proximity, alignment, repetition, and contrast leads to usable, accessible, and visually appealing web pages.
